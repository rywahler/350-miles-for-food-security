Pittsburgh to Washington, D.C.

This semester I biked home on the GAP and C&O Towpath trails as a capstone to my Junior year at Carnegie Mellon, raising over $5,000 for food banks in Maryland and Pittsburgh in the process. Starting the morning of April 30th headed out from downtown Pittsburgh with the goal of arriving in front of the Lincoln Memorial in Washington, D.C, the evening of May 1st.

About the ride

Biking the GAP and C&O trails has been a dream of mine since I started school in Pittsburgh. I figured I should put some meaning into the ride, I garnered donations from family, friends, and others each just a couple cents per mile to make it a worthwhile experience. The goal was to raise $10 per mile, which over approximately 350 miles comes to $3,500, but we exceeded that goal and raised nearly $15 dollars per mile.

Volunteering with the Maryland Food Bank has been some of my most rewarding experiences, including gleaning produce from local farms to packing and distribution at the food bank's warehouse. Riding through the neighborhoods of Pittsburgh I have seen a need to support the food banks here as well. In Maryland an increased cost of living and food in particular, leaves 40% of food insecure Marylanders working full time jobs and earning too much to qualify for federal food assistance. Pittsburgh has seen immense revitalization with the growth of its tech hub. But Pittsburgh is a city of neighborhoods, and while some have thrived in the revitalization, other neighborhoods have lagged with the loss of heavy industry.

Donating to the Maryland Food Bank or the Greater Pittsburgh Community Food Bank directly supports community food access in two places that matter deeply to me. Both organizations are highly effective in their work. The Maryland Food Bank has a 90% resource efficiency rate, and the Greater Pittsburgh Community Food Bank helps provide three meals for every dollar donated.
